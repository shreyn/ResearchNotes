## 4.1 Modular Arithmetic
### Congruence Modulo $n$
Two integers are **congruent modulo $n$** if: 
$$a \equiv b\mod n$$
- means $n | (a-b)$
#### Equivalence Classes
Congruence Modulo is an equivalence relation (R, S, T), so it **partitions integers**.
Ex: $n = 5$
- 0: ..., -5, 0, 5, ...
- 1: ..., -4, 1, 6, ...
**Integers modulo** $n$:
$$\mathbb{Z}_n = \{0,1,2, \dots, n-1\}$$
